THE CONTROLLER

Num Lock, short for "number lock," is a feature found on computer keyboards. When the Num Lock key is activated, the numeric keypad on the right side of the keyboard functions as a calculator-style numeric keypad, allowing users to enter numbers and perform calculations easily.

By activating Num Lock, the keys on the numeric keypad change their behavior. For example, pressing the keys with numbers will input the corresponding digits, and other keys on the numeric keypad, such as arithmetic operators (+, -, *, /), can be used for calculations.

The Num Lock key is typically located in the top left corner of the numeric keypad or in the vicinity of the Scroll Lock and Caps Lock keys. The state of the Num Lock key is indicated by an LED light on the keyboard, usually labeled "Num Lock" or with a lock symbol.

It's important to note that the Num Lock feature may vary depending on the keyboard and computer configuration. In some cases, the Num Lock key may be enabled or disabled by default when the computer starts up, and this behavior can often be customized through the computer's BIOS or operating system settings.
